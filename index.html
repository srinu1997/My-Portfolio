Why Surveillance is Important ?

Preventing and Reducing Crime Deterrence: Since potential criminals are less likely to conduct crimes if they are aware that they are being observed, the installation of surveillance cameras can help prevent criminal activity. Evidence Gathering: Surveillance film helps law enforcement identify and apprehend criminals, providing vital evidence in the investigation of crimes. Rapid Reaction: By enabling quicker incident reactions, real-time monitoring may be able to stop incidents from getting worse and causing less damage. 2. Public Safety Crowd Management: Surveillance aids in crowd control, upholding public order, and averting mishaps and incidents in public areas and at major events. Emergency Situations: By giving real-time information, surveillance systems can help manage and coordinate reactions to catastrophes, such as natural disasters or terrorist attacks. 3. Workplace Safety Monitoring: Monitoring takes place at places of business to make sure workers are safe. 4. Retail Theft Prevention: By keeping an eye on shoplifting and theft, surveillance in retail settings safeguards both customer and company interests. 5. Handling of Traffic: Traffic Surveillance: Traffic surveillance contributes to traffic flow monitoring, congestion control, and road safety. 6. Compliance with Privacy Protection Regulations: Ensuring that surveillance methods adhere to legal requirements helps safeguard people's right to privacy while preserving security. Accountability: By making people and organisations answerable for their deeds, surveillance can promote an environment of openness and accountability. 7. Developments in Technology Improved Capabilities: With the use of cutting-edge technology like artificial intelligence (AI) and facial recognition, modern surveillance systems provide improved security and monitoring capabilities. 8. Global Threats Counter-Terrorism: Monitoring and stopping possible terrorist activity is greatly aided by surveillance.
